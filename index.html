Introduction

This report provides a structured analysis of Louis Vuitton’s Autumn Winter 25 Women’s assortment in comparison with its main competitors. Each visualization highlights how portfolios are distributed by brand, category, and type, and where Louis Vuitton stands in relation to Hermès, Dior, and Saint Laurent. All of the following insights are generated from resourcefully gathered publicly available data.

This project highlights my skills in Python, Plotly, analytical reporting, and interactive website construction to show how I can support Louis Vuitton’s continued growth in the market.

Items per brand
Bar chart that compares item counts for each brand to understand assortment scale.
  • Louis Vuitton runs a focused range below Dior and above Saint Laurent.
  • Hermès holds the largest count which increases complexity to manage breadth.
  • Dior is similar in scale to Hermès but allocates items differently by category.
  • Saint Laurent holds the smallest range which limits assortment power.
  • LV benefits from easier tracking and tighter control of SKUs which supports quality and clear storytelling compared with Hermès and Dior.
Category overview
Treemap that shows contribution of brands and types to total items which reveals where portfolios are concentrated.
  • Hermès leans into Ready to Wear which raises exposure to fashion cycles.
  • Dior and Saint Laurent distribute more evenly across Leather Goods and RTW with main focus still on RTW.
  • Louis Vuitton centers on Bags which drives desirability and brand strength. Leather goods dominance echoes LV’s artisanal DNA.
  • LV should keep Bags leadership and build gradual presence in Jewelry and RTW to hedge volatility while retaining brand clarity.
    • Why it matters to LV?
    While bags are the most profitable category with high margins and strong brand association, RTW and Jewelry offer growth opportunities and help balance the portfolio against market fluctuations.

Share and type mix within category
Two charts that move together with the same category selection. Left shows brand share within the category and right shows the type mix inside that category. Interactive buttons to choose the category are applied.
  • LV protects category share leadership in Perfumes and Others, while other share varies with stronger rivals in apparel.
  • Hermès often rises in RTW which changes the competitive set.
  • Dior remains balanced which reduces exposure to single category swings.
  • Saint Laurent dominates apparel heavy types such as Jackets and Skirts.
  • Some brands miss entire type families which creates white space: Hermès in Leather Goods and Saint Laurent in Meshes.
  • Dior spreads coverage across many types with moderate intensity.
  • White space types represent areas where Louis Vuitton could expand its offer and gain new sales without reducing demand for its existing products.
LV should enter two or three RTW types such as Jackets and Pants with capsule depth to test lift while keeping collection focus.
Why it matters to LV?
Expanding into these types can attract new customers and enhance the brand's appeal without diluting its core identity. Ready-to-Wear is currently the weakest category in LV’s assortment compared to Hermès, Dior, and Saint Laurent. Introducing limited, high-impact capsule collections allows LV to strengthen credibility in apparel without diluting focus.

Brand versus category difference by mean
Small multiples show how each brand differs from the overall market mean for every category which highlights over and under representation.
  • LV seems to be fairly represented in all markets versus the market mean with the undersepresentation in RTW.
  • Hermès shows a pattern with weight in RTW being not represented in other categories.
  • Dior sits closer to averages which spreads risk, with underexposure to RTW.
  • Louis Vuitton appears balanced across most categories, with no extreme over or under representation, though it shows a clear weakness in Fashion and RTW where it is significantly underrepresented.
Final takeaways
    • Louis Vuitton leads in Leather Goods and maintains strong lifestyle pillars such as Perfumes and Special Objects (e.g., the Ukulele Case and Lantern Trunk). Perfumes and Special Objects extend the narrative beyond products into iconography.
    • Ready to Wear remains the main gap when compared with Hermès and Saint Laurent.
    • Louis Vuitton should sustain its Bags leadership, as Bags remain the brand’s strongest driver and the key item clients want to buy from a luxury house.
    • Piloting two or three focused RTW capsules in Jackets and Pants would bring the assortment closer to competitors while preserving brand focus.
    • Expanding Jewelry with measured depth could attract new customers and add breadth to the offer.
    • Developing capsule stories that connect Bags with Apparel will reinforce Bag desirability while lifting adjacent categories.
    • Considering Louis Vuitton’s new makeup line, which carries a premium price compared with competitors, linking the narrative between Bags and Beauty could encourage customers to engage across categories.
    • In conclusion, achieving a better balance across categories, while preserving clarity of identity, provides a clear path to share gains, stronger storytelling, and sharper differentiation from competitors.
